SEO & SMO Strategies

Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

Implemented SEO Strategies:

It happens that most SEO strategies work in the real world. One such example is before and after joining Nike, which increased the visibility of the website by optimizing it for use by voice assistants as the number of consumers continues rising with their voice assistants. Nike also redesigned its product descriptions and meta content to mimic human conversational queries. The increase from voice search by 30 per cent was seen suddenly reflected in online sales. This has shown the real business impact that can be made possible with the merging of SEO and technologies like voice search (PageTraffic, 2024).

An equally persuasive example of image-based SEO in user experience optimization would include its use by Sephora. Sephora has now set a page-search function, where users could upload images of the looks they want to achieve with the makeup. It can work on optimized image tags and structured data to draw relationships between uploaded images to relevant suggestions of products. Hence, Sephora has experienced a 15% increase in the conversion rate with brand engagement. These outcomes show how well aligned SEO with more innovative UX features can capture traffic and conversions. This is supported by PageTraffic (2024) and SEMrush (2024).

Among the most recent models of SEO success come out of Moz's strategic plan to optimize quality, user-focused content. Moz produced long guides and blog posts answering very specific questions that users generally asked about SEO tools and techniques. Optimized for targeted long-tail keywords, structured for featured snippets, and employing any other optimization technique, Moz thereafter succeeded in regularly attaining a "position zero" in search engine results. The approach yielded a 20% annual increase in organic traffic to the firm while at the same time solidified its leading stature on the SEO sector. It sends the success signals of combining useful information with keywords and snippets.

Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Implemented SMO Strategies:

    Currently, the most well-known and most efficient SMO strategies among all others is the video content strategy that aims to sign-on audiences. The short-form, immersively interactive videos are consolidated as a foundational element of social media strategy by these: Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. A general use of these two spaces called Instagram Reels and TikTok is for creating behind-the-scenes content, tutorials, and immersive storytelling segments for the corresponding audience. Nike and many other brands have leveraged such strategies, producing short, impactful, very empowering videos into the exact way of its advertising and its brand's narrative.

    The best defining case of SMO in action would take place where an Instagram campaign and a Twitter campaign is launched around the seasonal product event-such as the Pumpkin Spice event that Starbucks holds annually-by marketing specific products, in this case flavoring coffee products with what people expect from pumpkin spice. Then Starbucks will create initiatives around hashtags, and for this case we might have #PSL (from Pumpkin Spice Latte) and encourage user-generated content by some sort of invitation to customers to share their experiences. Following that, they will be engaging their followers tastefully via really enticing posts, polls, and stories. Thus, bringing visibility to the campaign, while building into the forming community at the brand level. It is believed that the campaign on social media site #PSL in 2023 has been a contributor to increasing engagement by over 25 percent and sales by approximately 10 percent in the area's fall-related products, all of which indicate how open, interactive, well-thought-out, and visually appealing SMO can become in the outdoors (Evergreen Brain, 2024; Oake Marketing, 2024).

    Airbnb created a community based on itself using a social media platform such as Facebook and Instagram. By using their hashtag, #AirbnbExperiences, it invites both hosts and guests to share their experiences so that the company can use UGC in those campaigns. The story has been redesigned and the individual stories will have to go through moderation before going onto the Airbnb site. This creates a sense of community and belonging while also allowing users to share their experiences on the platform. This approach lead to higher engagement and 15% increase in follower growth and engagement in 2023.
